Graphing is a method of representing and displaying coordinates(points) or functions in a graphical space.

Coordinate Plane

Graphing occurs in a coordinate plane. This means that it is a grid, and each location has a unique identifier. The horizontal solid line is the x-axis and the vertical one is the y-axis. Where they cross is called the origin. The origin's coordinates are \((0,0)\), meaning that its x value is 0 and its y value is 0.

Ordered Pair

An ordered pair is the name for the coordinates of a point. Each ordered pair has an x and y coordinate, in that order. Given the ordered pair \((x,y)\), its graph would have a point x boxes to the right of the origin and y boxes above the origin. Negative numbers would cause the point to be to the left or below the origin, respectively.

Lines

Lines are an infinite series of points extending in two directions. Lines are usually graphed with a function, which is a mathematical expression which gives a y value for every x value.

Distance Formula

It is possible to find the distance between any two points in a coordinate system by using the distance formula -

\[d=\sqrt{(x_2-x_1)^2+(y_2-y_1)^2}\]

- where the first point is at \((x_1,y_1)\) and the second is at \((x_2,y_2)\).
