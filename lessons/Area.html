Area is the amount of space contained in a closed shape. Because of its two-dimensional nature, area is measured in square units (square inches, sq ft, \(cm^2\)). Generally, area can only be found by the use of formulas specific to the shape being studied. Below are the formulas for common shapes.

Shape Area Formula
Rectangle \( A=l \times w \)
Parallelogram \( A = b \times h \)
Trapezoid \( A = \frac{1}{2} h ( b1 + b2 ) \)
Triangle \( A = \frac{1}{2} b \times h \)
Circle \( A = \pi r^2 \)
Ellipse (Oval) \( A = \pi r_1 r_2 \)

Surface area is the total area on the surface of a three-dimensional object. It is simply the sum of the areas of each face of the object.
